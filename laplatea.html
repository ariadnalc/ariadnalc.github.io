La Platea –
Bringing theatre
closer to young people

overview

For the digital experience design master’s thesis our team was in charge of designing an experience to people who consume or want to consume theatre. I worked alongside Ricardo Sánchez and Stephany Bernstein to create and develop La Platea, a web app to make easier and faster the process of searching and purchasing theater tickets in Barcelona. We develop the project following the Scrum-kanban Agile methodology.

Design Process

Research

Market Analysis

We started doing a deep research analysis of the theatre market in Barcelona. These are the results we found:

Data acquired from ADETCA (Empreses de teatre de Catalunya)

There was something we could do about the occupancy of the theaters. Barcelona is a big and cultural city with a wide range of performances, being highly valued by spectators. Despite the high advertisements and the discounts offered, the occupation of the theatres is only of 56%.

User interviews

To understand the low occupancy of the theaters, we did surveys to people from different backgrounds and ages to know why and how often they go to the theatre. We discovered that most people only go to theatre between 1 or 2 times a year and the reasons are due to the high prices or because they struggle to find something interesting to see.

We decided to focus on a young audience (between 25 and 35 y/o) who despite having the means to go and they want to, they usually don’t go.

To discover our users pain-points and social habits, we interviewed 25 people from different backgrounds. We focused the questions on the next points:

  1. 1. Reasons why they go to the theatre
  2. 2. Frustrations of their experience
  3. 3. The process from the awareness until leaving the theater

Building empathy

Personas

Following our user interviews, we built three personas depending on the needs that move them:

We decided to prioritize Anna because is the one who we can help more to improve her theatre experience and was the more common archetype in our interviews.

Alex is very familiar with the theatre world, the only pain-point he has is the registration and payment, he would like to haver an easier and faster experience.

Laura adapts herself to another person, she wants to go to theatre because a friend or a relative propose to her to go.

So, we focalize our empathy process on Anna.

Our primary persona

So, I introduce you Anna and her Journey Map, from where we could find the insights that helped us to build our product.

User Journey Map

Pain-Points and improvement opportunities

Based on the insights from our User Interviews, represented in the User Journey Map we extracted the main pain points:

Insight #1 – Our personas are not aware about new shows and opportunities.

Insight #2 – Our personas don’t know a site with all the theatre offer (and they would like it exists) to save time searching for a show.

Insight #3 – Our personas lack information and sometimes they get disappointed because they don’t see what they expected.

"What I hate the most is when I pay for something I don’t enjoy. Sometimes I feel I go “blindly” to the theatre and I don’t like to go to see a show without knowing what I am going to see."

Insight #4 – Our personas percieve theatre as something expensive, they search for discounts.

We exctracted also opportunities to improve their experience:

Op #1 – Our personas decide based on reviews but they don’t rate the shows.

Op #2 – Our personas say the register process is lenghtly and boring.

Op #3 – Despite our personas use their calendar to remember the day of the show, they would like to have a reminder.

Visual Language

We decided to use Montserrat as the main font-family, given its good legibility for body size but also warm and original character when used as titles.

For the colors, we wanted to transmit this magic and fantasy of the theatre as well as it’s tradition and elegancy. That’s why we combined the purple with the blue, two elegant and nighty colors. We complement this two dominant colors with a carefree pink for UI elements (CTA) and an elegant gold to transmit this traditional and luxurious feel.
